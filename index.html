Introduction

I'm a healthcare data analyst at the Bronx VA Medical Center starting to learn front-end development. The tools I commonly use at work are SQL Server Management Studio and Reporting Services, Pyramid Analytics, R, Looking Glass Clinical Analytics, and ArcGIS. I'm also a fifth-year doctoral student in Public Health at the City University of New York.

Medical errors and health disparities are issues that I'd like to try to address using health information technology. Since I started working in healthcare 20 years ago, electronic health records have changed very little. The Internet, cloud-based technologies, and SaaS have the potential to lead to real advances in healthcare, but it won't be easy for the small companies and independent developers; the market is dominated by a few giants. Even so, I remain optimistic!

Where I'm From

Originally I'm from Missouri Valley, a small town in Southwest Iowa, but I've spent my adult life in cities: Minneapolis, Washington, D.C., and for the last 10 years New York. What I love most about New York is the feeling that anything is possible here. The city is like a living creature; neighborhoods are always changing - new restaurants and shops popping up, fun food trends (hello food trucks and cronuts!), and more opportunities to see and hear art and music than you can shake a stick at. Currently I'm 10 years into a hopefully never-ending quest to find the best pizza in NYC.
